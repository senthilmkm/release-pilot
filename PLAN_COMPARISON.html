Who each plan is for

A quick decision guide based on your situation.

Free

  • Solo dev with your first app. Ship calmly with weekly checklists + occasional review replies.
  • Trying the app. Connect one ASC team, one app, kick the tires for as long as you want.
  • Don't need push or widgets. Happy refreshing ASC manually a few times a week.

Pro · Monthly FLEX

  • Testing the waters. Try Pro for a month, cancel anytime in Settings.
  • Shipping season only. Need Pro during a 6-week submission window, then pause.
  • Don't qualify for the trial. Used the yearly trial in the past — monthly is the next option.

Pro · Yearly BEST

  • Multi-app indie dev. Ship 2+ apps continuously and want push + widgets every day.
  • First-time Pro. 7-day free trial lets you fully validate before paying.
  • Want the best price. $3.33/mo equivalent vs $4.99 on monthly — 33% cheaper.
  • Lock in v1.0 pricing. Future price changes don't hit you until your next renewal.

Monthly vs Yearly — the only differences

Both Pro tiers unlock exactly the same features. The differences are purely commercial:

Pro · Monthly

  • Price: $4.99 / month
  • Cadence: 12 charges per year
  • Annual cost: $59.88
  • Free trial: No
  • Cancel friction: Lowest — you can drop after 1 month
  • Best for: Short-term Pro need (a release window, evaluating long-term)

Pro · Yearly

  • Price: $39.99 / year
  • Cadence: 1 charge per year
  • Annual cost: $39.99 (save $19.89)
  • Free trial: 7 days, new subscribers only
  • Cancel friction: Trivially — Settings → Subscriptions → Cancel. You keep Pro until end of paid period.
  • Best for: Anyone using Pro every day

How plan-switching works inside iOS

You can switch between Monthly and Yearly anytime — Apple handles the math:

  • Monthly → Yearly: Upgrade is immediate. Apple prorates the unused portion of your monthly bill as credit toward the yearly.
  • Yearly → Monthly: Downgrade takes effect at the end of your current yearly term. You keep Pro until then, then start being billed monthly.
  • Either Pro → Free: Cancel in iOS Settings. You stay Pro until your current billing period ends; on expiry the app drops to Free limits automatically.

The switcher lives in the app under More → Subscription → Change plan.

How the 7-day free trial works

Only on Pro · Yearly, only for new subscribers.

Eligibility

The 7-day trial is offered by Apple's StoreKit, not by Release Pilot. You're eligible if:

  • You've never started a Pro · Yearly trial for Release Pilot before, AND
  • Your Apple ID has not previously subscribed to release_pilot_pro_yearly

If you've used the trial in the past, the paywall will simply show the regular $39.99/year price with no trial — same product, just no intro offer.

The clock

The trial starts the moment you confirm the StoreKit sheet. You are not charged for 7 days. On day 7 your card is charged $39.99 and Pro · Yearly begins.

You can cancel anytime during the 7 days — iOS Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → Release Pilot → Cancel. You keep Pro features through the end of day 7, then drop to Free.

Frequently asked questions

Common questions about how the plans behave.

Is there a feature difference between Monthly and Yearly Pro?

No. They unlock identical features. Yearly is just cheaper per month and includes a 7-day trial for new subscribers.

What happens to my data if I downgrade from Pro to Free?

Nothing is deleted. Your connected ASC accounts, RevenueCat keys, and cached data all stay. But these Pro behaviors stop:

  • Push notifications no longer fire
  • Lock-Screen and Home-Screen widgets show empty state
  • Live Activities don't start for new submissions
  • Today tab stops refreshing RevenueCat metrics
  • Apps #2+ (alphabetically) and ASC accounts #2+ are paywalled when you try to interact with them
  • Checklist and review-reply quotas reset to Free limits

If you re-subscribe later, everything resumes immediately with no setup loss.

Why is the Today tab Pro-only?

The Today tab depends on you connecting your RevenueCat project, and connecting RevenueCat is itself a Pro gate. The reasoning: RC integration is a serious-dev signal — once you're tracking subscription revenue, you've usually crossed the line from hobby to business and Pro features pay for themselves immediately.

What about the 1-app limit on Free — which app does it pick?

Free users get full features for the alphabetically first app in their ASC account. App #2, #3, etc. show as "Pro required" — tapping them opens the paywall with a clear "Track all your apps" message.

Sort order is deterministic so the gating is consistent no matter what you tap first.

Can I switch from Yearly to Monthly mid-term?

Yes, but the change happens at the end of your current yearly period — not immediately. You keep Pro · Yearly until renewal, then start being billed $4.99/month from that day. Apple handles all of this; Release Pilot just reads your active subscription.

What if I'm in the 7-day trial and cancel?

You keep Pro features through the end of day 7 with no charge, then drop to Free on day 8. To cancel: iOS Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → Release Pilot → Cancel. Or in the app: More → Manage Subscription.

Do refunds happen if I cancel mid-cycle?

Apple handles all refunds — not Release Pilot. You can request a refund at reportaproblem.apple.com. Cancellation alone does not refund you; it just stops the next renewal.

Is there a Lifetime plan?

Not at launch. The app's pricing engine supports lifetime (and there's already code wired for it), but we want to validate the subscription pricing first. If demand is high we'll add lifetime in a future update.

Family Sharing?

Not in v1.0. May be enabled in a future update once we've validated the audience.

What's the "Pro · Lifetime" tier I see in the app code?

It's a tier we've reserved for a future lifetime IAP. Until we publish a lifetime product in ASC, no one can subscribe to it — even though the code recognizes it.
